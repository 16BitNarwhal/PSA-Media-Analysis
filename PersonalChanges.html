What I Would Change

If I were to film the PSA, I would change the ending and remove the cliffhanger as it doesn’t really help the viewers to visualize what might happen right after a applicant gets rejected. This could lower the emotions (sadness and anger) that can be used to help raise awareness and to support the NOAS campaign. Another change I would make is to add more stories instead of showing that many applicants being sad or scared because when you add a story with emotions, it can help trigger the need to support this campaign.

Final Notes

There are many other things I noticed, but didn’t know where to put. It is very clear that there are many strict regulations and that the judges (government) treat everything like a game. All the applicants have numbers on them (instead of actual names), they are only given 90 seconds to prove their reasoning for staying, and the judges like to laugh and make jokes in the serious situation. Also, when the judges don’t feel that the applicants should stay, they show no sign of sympathy and give stern looks. Although when the judges accept an applicant, they have a celebration as if everybody is well and happy even after the many sad stories and experiences the applicants had to talk about.
